Summary of the program to evaluate the results of primary school students in Southeast Asia

By Group5| Jan 28, 2021 | Article

On the morning of October 1, the Ministry of Education and Training (Education and Training) held a conference to review the program on assessing the learning outcomes of primary school students in Southeast Asia (SEA-PLM) for the 2018-2021 period. The conference was held in an online format with the participation of UNICEF representatives in Vietnam, representatives of the Departments of Education and Training, a number of universities and primary schools participating in the assessment program. Deputy Minister Nguyen Huu Do chaired the Conference.

Vietnamese students ranked first in 3 areas: Math, Reading and Writing SEA-PLM is a program that assesses 5th grade students in the areas of Mathematics, Literacy, Writing and Global Citizenship, emphasizing the quality and depth of ongoing learning in these areas. .

SEA-PLM serves the goal of improving the quality of education through systematic monitoring of student learning outcomes. At the same time, support SEAMEO member countries to better measure and understand student achievement as a whole and for individual groups by applying culturally appropriate metrics to learning outcomes. in Reading, Writing, Mathematics and Global Citizenship.

There are 6 countries, namely Cambodia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, the Philippines and Vietnam participating in the first survey cycle SEA-PLM in the period 2018-2021, the official survey in 2019. The results in 3 areas are Math, Reading Comprehension and Writing of Vietnam are the highest among the 6 countries participating in the official SEA-PLM 2019 survey.

Specifically, in the field of Mathematics, the average score of Vietnamese students is 341.55 points. Meanwhile, the average score of students from 6 countries is 304.79 points. In Reading Comprehension, the average score of Vietnamese students is 336.46 points. Meanwhile, the average score of students from 6 countries is 300 points. In Writing, the average score of Vietnamese students is 328.01 points. Meanwhile, the average score of students from 6 countries is 304.92 points.

Detailed analysis of survey results in Vietnam shows that students in remote areas are still far from students in other areas in terms of results in 3 areas. Female students' academic performance in the field of Mathematics was similar to that of male students, but in the areas of Reading and Writing, female students achieved higher results than male students.

Students from families with good economic conditions, with high average have a large difference in average scores and proficiency levels compared to students from families with low average economic conditions. The higher the education level of parents, the better the academic performance of their children. In addition, parents' occupations and learning conditions at home also have a great influence on students' learning outcomes.

With the participation in SEA-PLM, in addition to the common goals of ASEAN countries, Vietnam is aiming for a number of specific goals such as integration with other countries in the region in education; measuring the educational level of Vietnam compared to other countries in the region; analyze the current state of education and make recommendations to change education policies to improve the quality of education in Vietnam better and better.

In addition, through the SEA PLM, Vietnam demonstrates its responsibility for the construction and development of the common home of Southeast Asian countries. SEA PLM's assessment also serves the cause of Vietnam's general education innovation.

Talking at the conference, representatives of some Departments of Education and Training, educational institutions highly appreciated the meaning and importance of SEA-PLM in the period of 2018-2021 for quality assessment and identification of problems to be overcome. to improve the quality of local education.

“We are building an open, real, hands-on education that teaches well, learns well and manages well. Therefore, the positive results when compared with education in other countries in the region and in the world will not only be a joy for the education sector but also a joy for the whole country," emphasized the Deputy Minister.
